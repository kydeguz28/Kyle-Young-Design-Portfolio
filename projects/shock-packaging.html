Formula SAE Project

Shock Packaging

As the responsible engineer for Bruin Formula Racing shock packaging, I drove the subsystem from concept to production-ready hardware. The work was centered on packaging a complete shock assembly within the space available around the upright while preserving steering travel, brake clearance, and serviceability.

I built detailed SolidWorks models of the upright, control arm interfaces, and shock linkage, then iterated mounting brackets, rotational clearances, and hose routing to avoid interference. The design also incorporated adjustable damping access and durable hardpoints for repeated loading.

The finished package required close coordination with the chassis, brake, and steering teams to ensure the shock system fit the full vehicle architecture. I validated the layout with interference checks, assembly walkthroughs, and manufacturing reviews before releasing drawings for machined and welded components.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

FEA simulations were performed on critical mounting brackets and load-carrying components to verify structural integrity under dynamic racing loads. These analyses helped optimize bracket thickness, material selection, and weld geometry to ensure durability while minimizing weight.

Topology Optimization

Topology optimization was used to explore efficient bracket designs that maintained stiffness while reducing material and weight. The analysis guided the final geometry of mounting plates and load paths through the suspension structure.

Lug Analysis & Fastener Design

The shock linkage connects to the upright and control arm through welded lugs that experience complex multi-axial loading during cornering and braking. Detailed analysis of lug stress concentration, fastener preload, and bearing interaction ensured these critical joints could withstand the full operational envelope without fatigue cracking or slippage.
