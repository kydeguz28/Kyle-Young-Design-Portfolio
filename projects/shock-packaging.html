Formula SAE Project

Shock Packaging

As the responsible engineer for Bruin Formula Racing shock packaging, I led the subsystem from concept through manufacturable hardware. This work required balancing suspension geometry, upright clearance, brake packaging, serviceability, and interface with the steering system.

I modeled knuckle and control-arm interfaces in SolidWorks, iterated packaging around the vehicle frame, and coordinated with the chassis, brake, and steering teams to ensure reliable assembly. The final design emphasized serviceability, maintainable damping adjustments, and durable mounting under race loads.

Key contributions: defining packaging constraints, designing mounting details, partnering with manufacturing for DFM, and validating the layout with interference checks and assembly reviews.
