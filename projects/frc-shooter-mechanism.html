Robotics Project

FRC Shooter Mechanism

Led the mechanical design and integration of a competition robot shooter for SF Unity Robotics. The mechanism was developed under a six-week FRC build season and needed to deliver consistent shooting performance while surviving repeated match cycles.

I iterated prototypes for wheel spacing, hood geometry, and ball feed alignment, then selected actuation hardware and designed the mounting architecture for a durable, serviceable assembly. The shooter had to fit within the robot's limited packaging envelope and interface cleanly with the drivetrain and intake systems.

Final validation included tuning wheel speed, optimizing shot consistency, and ensuring the subsystem could be repaired quickly during events. The design helped the team maintain reliable performance across multiple competition matches.
