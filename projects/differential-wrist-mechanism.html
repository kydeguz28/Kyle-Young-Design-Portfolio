Technical Project

Differential Wrist Mechanism

Designed a custom end-effector wrist prototype as part 1 of a 5/6-axis robotic arm. The CAD and prototype were completed within two days of the project kickoff, and all components were custom 3D printed and finished on the machine shop lathe.

The mechanism is driven by two NEMA 17 stepper motors and controlled with an Arduino. I custom calculated the pulley and gear ratios to produce a compact wrist mechanism that uses a 1.25:1 reduction from power transmission to output.

This design was optimized for a small form factor while preserving torque and motion control. The differential wrist prototype demonstrates how careful ratio selection and differential gearing can shrink a stepper-driven wrist without sacrificing performance.

The completed build includes custom printed gearing, structural housings, and precision machined bearing surfaces, showing an end-to-end approach from rapid CAD iteration to prototype fabrication.
