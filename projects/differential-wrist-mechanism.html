Differential Wrist Mechanism

Designed a custom end-effector wrist prototype as part 1 of a 5/6-axis robotic arm. The CAD and prototype were completed within two days, and all parts were custom 3D printed and finished on the machine shop lathe.

The mechanism is driven by two NEMA 17 stepper motors and controlled by an Arduino. I calculated the pulley and gear ratios from scratch to create a compact differential wrist that uses a 1.25:1 reduction from power transmission to output.

The design focuses on small form factor, torque efficiency, and precise motion control. Differential gearing allowed the wrist to remain compact while providing clean stepper-based actuation and reliable output behavior.

The prototype includes custom printed gears and housings, integrated bearing supports, and detailed mounting features for a future multi-axis arm. It demonstrates a rapid engineering cycle from CAD to physical hardware.
